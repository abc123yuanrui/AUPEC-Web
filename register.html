Call for papers

Theme: Towards 100% Renewable Power and Electrified Transportation Systems

Within the last decade, electricity grid transformation has accelerated at a much greater rate nationally and globally as generation and load landscape shifts towards microgrids with distributed generation resources and more DC type systems driven by power electronics. All underpinning technologies aim to provide reliable, low-cost, emission-free, safe, efficient and effective power/energy conversion while we are reaching technical and operational limits in some areas, such as e-Mobility uptakes. AUPEC has been involved in every stage of the grid transformation. We are looking forward to welcoming you to the 32nd AUPEC2022 in Adelaide as we share our respective vision and perspectives on pathways towards 100% renewable power.

The scope of the conference includes but not limited to following topics:

  • Renewable Energy Resources
    • Solar energy
    • Wind energy
    • Hydro/tidal/wave energy
    • Geothermal energy
    • Integration of renewable resources to grid
    • Energy storage technologies & systems
  • Battery/EV chargers
    • DC-powered dwellings
    • Wide bandgap devices and applications
    • Energy conversion systems and applications
    • Geothermal energy
    • Component, converter and subsystem technologies
  • DC-Powered dwellings
  • Microgrids (DC, AC, and Hybrid)
  • e-Mobility and charging systems
  • Grid integration of electric vehicles
  • Electricity market and economics
  • Energy management and energy efficiency
  • Distribution, planning and operation
  • Computer-aided analysis of power systems
  • Islanded and remote grids
  • Distributed Energy Resources
  • Mine Electrification
  • Power system stability and control
  • Load and frequency control
  • Reactive power and voltage control
  • IoT and IoE in Power Systems
  • Intelligent control of power systems
  • Electrical machine and drives
  • Power Electronics
  • Wide Bandgap Devices and applications
  • Hydrogen and Fuel Cells
  • Nuclear Energy
